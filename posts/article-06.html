The Wrong Lab That Taught Me Responsibility

Muhammad Tahir Yaqoob | A small mistake with a big lesson

At the beginning of the semester, our class was divided into two groups, G1 and G2. I was assigned to Group 2. During one of the early lab sessions, confusion about the schedule caused several students, including me, to attend the wrong laboratory.

Instead of going to our AICTE lab, we went to the Programming Fundamentals lab with the other group. The session itself was useful and interesting because we learned how to install Visual Studio Code and set up the Python programming environment.

Unfortunately, we later realized that we had been in the wrong lab. Because of that, our attendance for the AICTE session was marked absent and marks for that lab were deducted.

One wrong room can teach you the importance of double-checking everything.

At first it felt frustrating, but the experience taught me something important. From that day onward, I became much more careful about schedules, announcements, and course instructions.
