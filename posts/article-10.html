Finishing the Semester at the Top

Muhammad Tahir Yaqoob | Project work, finals, and top result

The final project

As the semester neared its end, we were assigned a Programming Fundamentals project related to machine learning concepts. Together with my friend Zain, I developed a weather prediction model using historical weather data from Europe. The model attempted to predict temperature patterns and rainfall based on past data.

Although it was a small academic project, it was my first experience working with real data and applying programming concepts to a practical problem.

Presentation day pressure

When it was time to present the project, all of our files were on my laptop. The classroom projector required a wired connection, and when I connected my laptop, the projector simply would not recognize it. For a moment, it felt like the entire presentation might collapse.

We quickly transferred our code, data, and presentation files to another student's laptop that connected successfully. That allowed us to continue.

Even during execution, the project created tension. Our dataset was relatively large, and the algorithm needed two to three minutes to produce output. The room became quiet, but we used that time to explain the dataset structure and our approach. Eventually the output appeared, and the presentation was completed successfully.

Programming projects rarely go perfectly, but problem-solving under pressure is part of the learning experience.

The result

Final exam preparation was intense, but the effort paid off. I scored over 90 marks in Programming Fundamentals and also achieved the highest marks across both sections in Discrete Mathematics.

When the semester results were announced, I achieved a GPA of 3.94 and became the top student of my first semester in Computer Engineering.
