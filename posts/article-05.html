Dr. Bilal's First Lecture: Skills Over Labels

Muhammad Tahir Yaqoob | A mindset shift

One of the most memorable early experiences in my university life came from the first lecture of our Programming Fundamentals instructor, Dr. Bilal.

Instead of beginning with technical slides, he started with a casual conversation about university life, hostel struggles, and the transition students face when moving away from home for the first time. That relaxed tone made the class feel comfortable.

Then he asked a question that caught everyone's attention: did we know the difference between PEC and NCEAC accreditation in Pakistan? Many students, including me, were unsure. He explained that some programs are recognized by the Pakistan Engineering Council while others fall under computing accreditation bodies.

“In the real world, people do not ask about the label of your degree. They ask what you can build.”

That statement stayed with me. It changed the way many of us thought about our studies. Instead of worrying about labels, the focus shifted toward building real skills. Programming no longer felt like only a subject to pass. It felt like a tool that could let us create something meaningful.

For me, that first lecture replaced uncertainty with motivation.
