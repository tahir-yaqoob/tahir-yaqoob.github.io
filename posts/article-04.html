Programming Was a Foreign Language

Muhammad Tahir Yaqoob | First steps in coding

When I entered the Computer Engineering program at UET Lahore Faisalabad campus, I quickly realized that I had almost no real programming background. Unlike some classmates who had studied computing before, I had come from an FSc Pre-Engineering background focused on mathematics, physics, and theory.

Programming felt completely different. It did not feel like just another subject. It felt like learning an entirely new language.

During the early classes of Programming Fundamentals, terms like loops, variables, and functions were being discussed. I remember sitting there wondering what these words even meant in a programming context. At that time, I honestly did not even understand something as basic as what a loop really did.

I did not know what a loop was, but the class still sparked my curiosity for the first time.

Something interesting happened, though. Even while it felt unfamiliar, it also pulled me in. Programming started to feel less like a foreign concept and more like a puzzle waiting to be solved.

The subject that once seemed intimidating gradually became the one I was most curious about. Every lecture revealed a little more logic and structure. That curiosity became the real starting point of my programming journey.
