Learning Responsibility in University Life

Muhammad Tahir Yaqoob | Independence and family expectations

University life was very different from college. For the first time in my life, I was living in a hostel away from home. Although I was not completely alone because my friend Muhammad Zain was also enrolled in Computer Engineering, the environment still felt unfamiliar.

Another friend from my FSc class, Ali Shirazi, was in the same hostel as well, even though he was studying Chemical Engineering. Having familiar faces around made the transition easier.

Family standards and motivation

At the same time, I felt a strong sense of responsibility from my family. My elder brother is a doctor who graduated with distinction and received a gold medal for academic excellence. My sister had also been a top student in her university.

I knew I had to prove myself, not out of fear, but because I wanted to live up to the standards set by my family.

Instead of discouraging me, that pressure slowly became a source of motivation. It gave me an inner push to take my studies seriously and show that I could also achieve something meaningful.
