About Me

My path into computer engineering started with uncertainty and became a story of discipline, growth, and belief.

I came from an FSc Pre-Engineering background, explored university options with limited clarity, and eventually chose Computer Engineering because it matched my natural curiosity for how computers actually work. My journey has been shaped by family expectations, supportive teachers, strong friendships, and moments that tested my confidence.

What Drives Me

I want to become an engineer who can build meaningful systems, not just collect grades. My journey taught me that labels matter less than skills, and that growth often begins where confidence is weakest.

Journey Timeline

Researching the Right University

After scoring above 85% overall in matriculation and FSc Pre-Engineering, I explored engineering universities in Pakistan and chose UET Lahore as my main target.

ECAT and Early Lessons

My first ECAT result gave me confidence, but the second attempt became a reality check. It taught me how preparation and decision-making can shape outcomes.

Choosing Computer Engineering

After comparing Computer Science and Computer Engineering, and speaking with a senior student, I chose the path that matched my interest in hardware and systems.

Beginning at Faisalabad Campus

The campus felt unfamiliar at first, but that difficult beginning gradually turned into a place of learning, adaptation, and meaningful progress.

Programming Changed My Confidence

Although programming first felt like a foreign language, it quickly became the subject that pushed me to grow. My quiz, midterm, and final results proved that I belonged here.

Turning Curiosity into Achievement

By the end of the semester, I had earned a GPA of 3.94 and achieved top standing. More importantly, I discovered what persistence could do.
