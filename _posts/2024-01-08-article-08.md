# Preparing for My First Programming Fundamentals Quiz

As the first semester progressed, I gradually became more comfortable with university life and my academic routine. The initial confusion of adjusting to a new environment had started to fade, and I was becoming more familiar with my courses and expectations. However, one challenge was approaching that made many students nervous: the first quiz of Programming Fundamentals.

Programming Fundamentals was one of the most important courses of the semester because it carried three credit hours and served as the foundation for future computing and engineering subjects. Since I had entered university without a strong programming background, I viewed the upcoming quiz as an important opportunity to test my understanding and measure my progress.

As soon as the quiz was announced, my friends and I began preparing seriously. Unlike school tests that sometimes required simple memorization, this quiz demanded actual understanding of programming concepts. We needed to understand logic, syntax, problem-solving techniques, and the practical application of ideas that we had learned during lectures and lab sessions.

Many evenings were spent studying together. My friend Muhammad Zain played an important role during this period because he already had some prior exposure to coding. Whenever I struggled with a concept, he would explain it from a practical perspective, which often made it easier to understand. Studying with friends created a positive learning environment where everyone helped each other improve.

We spent long hours reviewing lecture notes, practicing programming exercises, and discussing concepts that appeared difficult. One of the most useful resources during our preparation was the material shared by Dr. Bilal Ahmad, including his Hands-On Python notes. These notes helped simplify many concepts and provided practical examples that made learning easier.

Since we were learning Python as part of Programming Fundamentals, much of our preparation involved understanding how programs work rather than simply memorizing definitions. We practiced writing simple programs, tracing program execution, and identifying mistakes in code. At first, some of these activities seemed challenging, but repeated practice gradually improved my confidence.

The days leading up to the quiz were filled with both excitement and nervousness. Like many students, I wanted to perform well, but I also understood that this would be one of my first opportunities to evaluate how effectively I had adapted to university-level studies. The quiz felt important because it represented more than just marks. It represented progress.

# The Quiz Result That Surprised Me

When the day of the quiz arrived, I felt prepared but still uncertain about the outcome. I had worked hard and spent significant time studying, yet I knew there were many talented students in the class, including some who already had programming experience before joining university.

As I attempted the quiz, I focused on applying what I had learned rather than worrying about the result. The questions tested concepts that we had practiced repeatedly during our preparation sessions. Although I felt reasonably confident while solving them, I had no idea how my performance compared to that of other students.

After submitting the quiz, I tried not to think about it too much. Like most students, I simply waited for the results and hoped for the best.

When the results were finally announced, something happened that I never expected.

Only three students in the entire class achieved full marks, and I was one of them.

For a few moments, I could hardly believe it. I knew I had prepared seriously, but I had not anticipated scoring full marks. Seeing my name among the students who achieved the highest score was one of the most satisfying academic moments of my first semester.

> Getting full marks in that first quiz gave me confidence I had never felt before.

The result was important for reasons that went beyond the marks themselves. Before joining university, I sometimes worried that students with prior programming experience would always have a major advantage. Since I was still relatively new to programming, I occasionally questioned whether I would be able to compete academically.

That quiz result challenged those doubts.

It showed me that while prior experience is certainly helpful, dedication and consistent effort can also produce excellent results. The hours spent studying, practicing, and discussing concepts with friends had made a real difference.

I still remember the excitement that followed the announcement. My friends congratulated me, and I felt a sense of accomplishment that motivated me to continue working hard. For the first time, I genuinely began to believe that I could succeed in Computer Engineering if I remained committed to learning.

# How That Quiz Changed My Mindset

Looking back today, I believe the most valuable part of that experience was not the score itself but the confidence that came with it. Confidence can have a significant impact on academic performance because it influences how students approach challenges.

Before that quiz, I often approached programming with uncertainty. Whenever I encountered a difficult concept, I sometimes wondered whether my lack of prior experience would limit my progress. After the quiz, my perspective began to change.

I realized that learning is a process, and improvement comes through effort. Every student starts somewhere. Some may begin with more experience, while others start from the basics. What ultimately matters is the willingness to learn, practice, and persist when challenges arise.

This lesson influenced my approach throughout the remainder of the semester. Instead of focusing on what I did not know, I started focusing on what I could learn next. Difficult concepts became opportunities rather than obstacles. Mistakes became part of the learning process rather than reasons to lose confidence.

The experience also strengthened my appreciation for collaborative learning. Studying with friends, discussing ideas, and helping each other understand difficult topics proved extremely valuable. Success was not achieved in isolation. It was supported by a positive learning environment where students encouraged one another to improve.

Programming Fundamentals, taught by Dr. Bilal Ahmad, became one of the courses that helped shape my academic confidence during the first semester. Beyond teaching programming concepts, the course demonstrated the importance of consistent effort, logical thinking, and problem-solving. These skills are valuable not only in programming but throughout engineering education.

Another lesson I learned from this experience is that success often arrives after sustained effort rather than sudden inspiration. The full marks did not happen because of luck. They were the result of preparation, practice, and determination. That realization made the achievement even more meaningful.

As I continue documenting my journey through this GitHub portfolio blog, I consider this quiz one of the important milestones of my first semester. It was not the largest achievement of my academic career, but it was one of the first moments that genuinely boosted my confidence.

Most importantly, it taught me that hard work can compete with background knowledge. Students who begin with less experience should never underestimate what they can achieve through dedication and persistence. For me, that first Programming Fundamentals quiz became proof that effort truly matters.

The confidence I gained from that experience stayed with me long after the quiz ended. It encouraged me to approach future challenges with a more positive mindset and reminded me that success is often built one small achievement at a time.
