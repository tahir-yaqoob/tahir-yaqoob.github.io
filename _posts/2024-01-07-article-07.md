# Adjusting to Hostel Life and a New Environment

University life was very different from college. For the first time in my life, I was living in a hostel away from home. Until then, I had always been surrounded by family, familiar routines, and the comfort of a known environment. Joining UET Lahore Faisalabad Campus as a BS Computer Engineering student meant stepping into a completely new phase of life.

The transition was exciting, but it was not always easy. Everything around me felt unfamiliar. I had to adapt to a new campus, new classmates, different teaching styles, and a daily routine that was completely different from what I had experienced before. At first, even simple tasks such as managing my schedule, organizing my studies, and balancing responsibilities required extra effort.

Fortunately, I was not entirely alone during this transition. One of my close friends, Muhammad Zain, was also enrolled in the Computer Engineering program. Having a familiar face around made a significant difference. Whenever university life felt overwhelming, it was comforting to have someone who understood the same challenges and experiences.

Another friend from my FSc class, Ali Shirazi, was also living in the same hostel. Although he was studying Chemical Engineering instead of Computer Engineering, his presence helped make the hostel environment feel less unfamiliar. We often shared conversations about university life, academic challenges, and our experiences adapting to a new environment.

These friendships reminded me of the importance of having a support system. University can feel intimidating during the first few weeks, but having trusted friends nearby makes the transition smoother. Whether it was discussing assignments, navigating campus, or simply sharing daily experiences, those friendships helped reduce the stress that often comes with major life changes.

Living in a hostel also taught me independence. Unlike life at home, there was nobody constantly reminding me about responsibilities. I had to manage my own time, plan my studies, and ensure that I remained organized. While these responsibilities seemed challenging at first, they gradually helped me become more disciplined and self-reliant.

As the weeks passed, I became more comfortable with hostel life. The environment that initially felt unfamiliar slowly started feeling normal. I learned how to manage my daily routine, balance academic responsibilities, and adapt to the freedom that comes with university life. Looking back now, I realize that this adjustment period played an important role in my personal growth.

# Family Standards and the Motivation to Succeed

While adapting to university life, I was also carrying a strong sense of responsibility toward my family. Education has always been valued highly in our household, and I grew up seeing examples of academic excellence from those closest to me.

My elder brother is a doctor who graduated with distinction and received a gold medal for academic excellence. Throughout my life, I have admired the dedication, discipline, and hard work that helped him achieve such success. His accomplishments became an example of what is possible when someone remains focused on their goals.

My sister also performed exceptionally well during her university studies and consistently demonstrated a strong commitment to education. Seeing both of them succeed created an environment where academic achievement was respected and encouraged.

As I entered university, I became increasingly aware of these family standards. Naturally, I began comparing my own journey with theirs. There were moments when I wondered whether I would be able to meet similar expectations and achieve meaningful success in my own field.

> I knew I had to prove myself, not out of fear, but because I wanted to live up to the standards set by my family.

That feeling was not based on pressure in a negative sense. My family never forced unrealistic expectations upon me. Instead, it came from a personal desire to demonstrate that I could also achieve something worthwhile through dedication and hard work.

Rather than discouraging me, those standards gradually became a source of motivation. Whenever I felt tired, distracted, or uncertain, I reminded myself of the effort that my family members had invested in their own education. Their achievements served as evidence that consistent hard work eventually produces meaningful results.

This motivation became particularly important during the first semester. University studies required a higher level of commitment than I had previously experienced. Courses demanded more independent learning, assignments required greater effort, and examinations tested not only knowledge but also understanding and application.

During challenging moments, I often reflected on the examples set by my family. Instead of viewing their success as something intimidating, I began viewing it as inspiration. Their achievements showed me that success is not the result of talent alone. It comes from persistence, discipline, and a willingness to continue working even when progress feels slow.

# Finding My Own Path Forward

As the semester progressed, I gradually developed a clearer understanding of my own goals and responsibilities. While my family's achievements motivated me, I also realized that every person's journey is different. My objective was not to copy someone else's path but to create my own.

This realization helped me approach university with a healthier mindset. Rather than constantly comparing myself to others, I started focusing on personal improvement. Small achievements became important milestones. Completing assignments successfully, understanding difficult concepts, and performing well in quizzes all became signs of progress.

I also began appreciating the opportunities available to me as a Computer Engineering student. The field combines problem-solving, technology, and innovation in ways that can create meaningful real-world impact. Through courses such as Programming Fundamentals, I started developing skills that would form the foundation of my future studies.

Programming Fundamentals, taught by Dr. Bilal Ahmad, introduced me to logical thinking and problem-solving through Python. Beyond classroom concepts, the course helped me understand that learning technology is not simply about writing code. It is about developing the ability to analyze problems and create solutions. These lessons strengthened my confidence and encouraged me to take my studies more seriously.

Students interested in learning more about his professional and academic work can explore **[Dr. Bilal Ahmad's LinkedIn profile](https://www.linkedin.com/in/drbilalphd/)**.

Another important lesson I learned during the semester was that growth takes time. Success does not happen instantly, and meaningful achievements are usually the result of consistent effort over long periods. There were days when studying felt difficult and progress seemed slow. However, I gradually learned to trust the process and remain patient.

Creating this GitHub portfolio blog is also part of that journey. Inspired by the encouragement of Dr. Bilal Ahmad, I wanted to document my experiences, lessons, and growth as a Computer Engineering student. These articles allow me to reflect on my journey while leaving a digital footprint that represents my academic development.

Looking back, the transition from college to university taught me much more than academic content alone. Hostel life taught me independence. Friendships taught me the value of support and companionship. Family standards taught me motivation and perseverance. Together, these experiences helped shape my mindset during the first semester.

As I continue my journey in Computer Engineering, I remain grateful for the lessons learned during those early months. They helped me become more responsible, more motivated, and more confident in my ability to overcome challenges. Most importantly, they reminded me that success is not about competing with others. It is about becoming a better version of yourself through continuous learning and growth.
