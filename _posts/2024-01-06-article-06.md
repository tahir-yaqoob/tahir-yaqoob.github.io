# The Wrong Lab That Taught Me Responsibility

The first semester of university is full of new experiences, challenges, and adjustments. As a BS Computer Engineering student at UET Lahore Faisalabad Campus, I quickly realized that university life is very different from school. Every week introduced new courses, new classrooms, different laboratory sessions, and responsibilities that students were expected to manage on their own. During those early days, I was still learning how everything worked, and one small mistake taught me an important lesson that I still remember today.

At the beginning of the semester, our class was divided into two groups: G1 and G2. I was assigned to Group 2. Since the semester had just started, many students were still trying to understand their schedules and laboratory allocations. There were multiple announcements, different venues, and several changes that required careful attention. Like many first-semester students, I was trying my best to keep track of everything while also adjusting to university life.

During one of the early laboratory sessions, confusion about the schedule caused several students, including me, to attend the wrong lab. Instead of going to our AICTE laboratory, we went to the Programming Fundamentals lab session that was scheduled for the other group. At that moment, we genuinely believed we were in the correct place. Because several classmates were heading there as well, it seemed reasonable to follow them without double-checking the details ourselves.

Although we were in the wrong lab, the session itself was extremely useful. It was one of our early Programming Fundamentals practical classes, where we learned how to install Visual Studio Code and configure the Python programming environment. Since Python was the programming language we were using in Programming Fundamentals, this was an important step in beginning our programming journey.

Before joining university, I had limited experience with professional programming tools. Watching the instructor demonstrate Visual Studio Code and explain how to run Python programs felt exciting. I carefully followed every step, making sure that the software was installed correctly and that my environment was properly configured. Like many beginners, I felt a sense of achievement when my first simple Python program executed successfully.

At that stage of the semester, even small accomplishments felt significant. Running a basic program, understanding how an integrated development environment works, and becoming familiar with programming tools gave me confidence. I left the lab feeling that I had learned something valuable and useful for the weeks ahead.

Unfortunately, we later discovered that we had attended the wrong laboratory session.

Our actual scheduled session had been the AICTE lab, not the Programming Fundamentals lab. Because we were absent from the correct session, our attendance was marked accordingly. In addition, marks associated with that laboratory activity were deducted. The realization was disappointing because we had genuinely attended a lab and participated actively. However, the university system could only record attendance for the session we were officially assigned to attend.

At first, I felt frustrated. It seemed unfair because the mistake had not been intentional. We had spent the time learning and participating rather than skipping class. However, after thinking about the situation more carefully, I realized that the experience contained an important lesson.

> One wrong room can teach you the importance of double-checking everything.

That simple incident taught me that responsibility often involves paying attention to details that seem small at the time. In university, nobody constantly reminds students where they need to be or what they should do next. Students are expected to manage their own schedules, verify information, and take responsibility for their decisions.

Looking back, I understand that I relied too heavily on assumptions. Instead of personally verifying the laboratory schedule, I followed what others appeared to be doing. While that may seem harmless, it demonstrated how easily mistakes can happen when we fail to confirm information ourselves.

After that experience, I became much more careful about checking schedules, announcements, classroom locations, and laboratory timings. Whenever a new notification appeared on the university portal or in a class group, I made sure to read it carefully. Before attending any laboratory session, I would verify the room number, timing, and group allocation. This habit helped me avoid similar mistakes throughout the rest of the semester.

The lesson extended beyond academics as well. I realized that responsibility is not only about completing assignments and studying for exams. It also involves organization, attention to detail, and personal accountability. Small mistakes can sometimes have larger consequences than expected, especially when important information is overlooked.

As the semester progressed, I noticed that this experience made me more independent. Instead of relying on others for information, I started developing the habit of confirming things myself. Whether it was checking deadlines, reviewing course requirements, or preparing for laboratory sessions, I became more proactive in managing my academic responsibilities.

One interesting aspect of this experience is that it happened during a Programming Fundamentals laboratory session. Programming itself requires careful attention to detail. A small mistake in code, such as a missing bracket or incorrect variable name, can prevent an entire program from working correctly. In many ways, the lesson I learned from attending the wrong lab was similar to the lesson programming teaches. Small details matter, and success often depends on checking things carefully.

During our Programming Fundamentals course, Dr. Bilal Ahmad often encouraged students to develop strong problem-solving habits. While the wrong-lab incident was not directly related to programming concepts, it reinforced the importance of being careful, systematic, and responsible. These qualities are valuable not only in academics but also in engineering and professional life.

Today, when I think back to my first semester, this incident stands out because of the lesson it taught me. At the time, I viewed it as an unfortunate mistake. Now I see it as an important learning experience that helped me grow. Sometimes the most valuable lessons do not come from lectures, textbooks, or examinations. They come from everyday situations that teach us how to become more responsible and mature individuals.

Although losing attendance and marks was disappointing, the experience ultimately had a positive impact on me. It taught me to verify information, pay attention to details, and take ownership of my responsibilities. Those habits continue to help me in my academic journey today.

As a student building a GitHub portfolio to document my growth as a Computer Engineering student, I believe it is important to share not only achievements but also mistakes and lessons learned along the way. Growth often comes from challenges, and sometimes a simple misunderstanding can become a meaningful learning experience. For me, attending the wrong laboratory was one of those experiences.
