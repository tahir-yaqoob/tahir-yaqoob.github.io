# Ramadan and a Different Academic Routine

As the second semester continued, the holy month of Ramadan began. Like many students, I had to adjust my daily routine to balance fasting, worship, academic responsibilities, and hostel life. Ramadan always creates a different atmosphere in university life because students and teachers alike must manage their schedules around fasting and religious activities.

During this period, the pace of lectures became slightly slower. Teachers understood that students were fasting throughout the day, and students appreciated the flexibility that allowed everyone to manage their responsibilities more comfortably. Although classes continued normally, there was a noticeable effort to make academic activities more manageable.

At the same time, my classmates and I were preparing for our first quizzes of the semester. Courses such as Database Systems, Object-Oriented Programming, Digital Logic Design, and Calculus II required regular preparation, so maintaining a balance between academics and Ramadan became an important challenge.

Many students, including me, often preferred studying after Iftar when energy levels were higher and concentration improved. The hostel environment also became quieter during the evenings, creating a better atmosphere for revision and preparation.

Despite these adjustments, the semester was progressing smoothly. However, an unexpected situation soon disrupted the routine that students had established.

Around that period, international tensions in the region became a major topic of discussion. News channels continuously reported developments, and concerns about transportation and fuel availability began affecting daily life. As uncertainty increased, many institutions started exploring alternative ways of continuing their operations.

Eventually, educational activities also began shifting toward online systems, creating a situation that none of us had expected at the beginning of the semester.

# Returning Home During Online Classes

As transportation concerns increased, our university announced that classes would temporarily be conducted online. Students were advised to return to their homes and continue their studies remotely.

For many students, including me, this announcement created mixed feelings.

On one hand, returning home meant spending time with family during Ramadan, which was something I welcomed. On the other hand, it meant leaving behind the routine that I had established at the hostel and adapting to an entirely different mode of learning.

After making the necessary preparations, I packed my belongings and began the journey home. Unfortunately, the timing was not ideal because online classes had already started.

While traveling by train, I experienced several internet connectivity issues. Mobile signals frequently became weak or disappeared entirely as the train moved through different areas. Because of these problems, I was unable to attend some of the online lectures successfully.

As a result, I unfortunately missed two or three classes during the journey.

At the time, I felt frustrated because I had worked hard throughout the semester and did not want to miss important academic content. Unlike physical classrooms where attendance is straightforward, online learning depends heavily on stable internet access and reliable technology.

The experience reminded me that educational opportunities are often connected to factors beyond a student's control. Even when students are motivated and willing to learn, technical limitations can sometimes create unexpected obstacles.

Fortunately, once I completed the journey and arrived home, the situation improved significantly.

# Adapting to Online Learning

After finally reaching home, I quickly settled into a new academic routine. Since classes were now being conducted online, I needed to create a suitable study environment where I could attend lectures without interruption.

Starting from March 19, I attended online classes regularly and made every effort to stay updated with coursework. Although online learning felt different from the traditional classroom environment, it also offered several advantages. Students could attend lectures from their homes, access digital materials more easily, and save time that would otherwise be spent traveling between locations.

At the same time, online education introduced new challenges. Maintaining concentration during virtual lectures required greater self-discipline. Technical issues occasionally disrupted communication, and students had fewer opportunities for direct interaction with classmates and instructors.

Despite these difficulties, both students and teachers adapted surprisingly quickly. Within a short period, online classes became part of the daily routine. Assignments, lectures, discussions, and academic activities continued through digital platforms, demonstrating how flexible modern education systems can be when faced with unexpected circumstances.

Initially, the university announced that online classes would continue until March 31. However, there was uncertainty regarding whether that period would be extended. Many students regularly discussed possible future announcements and wondered when normal campus activities would resume.

> Although the situation was challenging, it showed how quickly education systems can adapt during unexpected circumstances.

Looking back, this period taught me several valuable lessons. First, adaptability is an essential skill for students. Academic plans do not always unfold exactly as expected, and being able to adjust to changing circumstances is important for success.

Second, technology plays a crucial role in modern education. Without online learning platforms, continuing classes during such a situation would have been much more difficult. The experience helped me appreciate how digital tools can support learning even when traditional classrooms are unavailable.

Finally, the transition reminded me that education is not limited to physical buildings. Whether in a classroom, a laboratory, a hostel room, or at home, learning can continue when students remain committed to their goals.

As I continued my second semester from home, I realized that unexpected challenges often become valuable learning experiences. Although the circumstances were unusual, they strengthened my ability to adapt, remain focused, and continue progressing despite uncertainty.
