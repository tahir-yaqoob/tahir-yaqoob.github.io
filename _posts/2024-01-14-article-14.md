The month of Ramadan has always felt special to me, but the Ramadan of my second semester at UET Lahore Faisalabad Campus became memorable for reasons I never expected. What started as a normal university routine gradually transformed into one of the most unusual periods of my academic life. Within a matter of days, I went from preparing for quizzes in the hostel to packing my belongings and traveling home as classes moved online.Looking back now, it feels like a period filled with uncertainty, unexpected changes, and experiences that I still remember clearly today.

### Ramadan Life in the Hostel

During Ramadan , daily life in the hostel changed significantly. Our schedules revolved around Sehri, fasting hours, classes, and Iftar. Since the hostel mess was not particularly good during Ramadan, many students developed their own routines for Sehri.My roommates and I usually woke up about an hour before Sehri ended. Rather than depending completely on the mess, we often prepared simple meals ourselves. One of our common Sehri meals consisted of leftover bread or parathas from Iftar, lightly fried and eaten with yogurt.It was not a luxurious meal, but it was enough to get through the fasting day.

One of the funniest memories from that period involved a friend from another hall. Whenever students complained about the quality of hostel food, he would argue that everyone was exaggerating. According to him, students simply enjoyed complaining and the food could not possibly be as bad as people claimed.One morning, he joined us for Sehri.We offered him the same paratha that we had been eating. Within moments, his confidence disappeared.After taking a few bites, he admitted that the food was far worse than he had imagined. The same person who had previously defended the mess food refused to continue eating it. We all laughed because he had spent weeks arguing that students were overreacting.That particular Sehri became unforgettable because four students ended up sharing only about three and a half parathas. It was not the most filling meal, but it became one of the funniest memories of my hostel life.

> Sometimes the most memorable university moments come from the simplest situations.

Apart from the food situation, Ramadan also changed the atmosphere of the hostel. Students stayed awake later at night, many spent time studying after Taraweeh, and conversations often continued long after midnight. Despite the academic workload, there was a sense of community that made the experience enjoyable.

As Ramadan continued, students were busy preparing for classes, assignments, and quizzes. At that time, an Object-Oriented Programming quiz was scheduled for the next day.Interestingly, that quiz had already been postponed before, and many students were not particularly excited about taking it. Like most students, I was preparing for it, but I certainly would not have minded having more time.Then, shortly before Iftar, everything changed.Around 4 PM, a notification began circulating through WhatsApp groups. Our Digital Logic Design teacher shared an announcement advising students to prepare to return home because the university was being affected by the ongoing regional situation and transportation concerns.

At first, many students could hardly believe it.Within a short time, however, the news spread across the hostel. Conversations immediately shifted from quizzes and assignments to travel plans and transportation arrangements.The reaction among students was mixed.Many students were secretly happy because the next day's OOP quiz would no longer take place. Since the quiz had already been delayed multiple times, the announcement almost felt unreal.Others were not nearly as happy.Some students had returned from home only a few hours earlier. After spending time and money traveling back to university, they suddenly learned that the university was closing and they would need to return home again.Looking back, their frustration was completely understandable.

> One unexpected notification completely changed everyone's plans.

Although people discussed the international situation and the transportation difficulties being reported across the country, most students were focused on a much simpler question: how quickly could they get home?

### Preparing for the Journey Home

Although I was happy to be returning home, I also felt uncertain about what would happen next. The university had announced online classes, which meant that academic activities would continue despite the closure.Unlike a normal vacation, this was not really a break.We still had lectures to attend, assignments to complete, and quizzes to prepare for.Because the announcement arrived close to our train departure time, there was no practical way for me to leave immediately. Instead, I packed my belongings and prepared to travel the following morning.Most of the students traveling with me were fellow Computer Engineering students, including several close friends. Since we were all dealing with the same situation, having familiar company made the journey easier.

Packing felt different from a normal trip home.Usually, when leaving the hostel, I knew approximately when I would return. This time, nobody knew exactly how long the situation would last. Some students believed the closure would only continue for a week or two. Others thought it might last much longer.The uncertainty made everything feel unusual.

The next morning, we left for the railway station.This journey was particularly memorable because it was only the second time in my life that I had traveled by train.Bus fares had increased significantly, so the train seemed like the most practical option. Although I had heard many stories about train travel, actually experiencing it felt different.The journey itself was not unpleasant.The main inconvenience was the dust, which seemed to find its way everywhere. Other than that, I actually enjoyed many parts of the experience. Whenever seats or benches became available, I would stretch out and rest for a while.Traveling with friends also made the trip much more enjoyable. Conversations about university life, upcoming online classes, and future plans helped pass the time.However, one challenge remained unavoidable.

Online classes had already started.Because internet connectivity was weak on the train and inconsistent at several railway stations, I missed multiple lectures during the journey. Every time a signal appeared, I would quickly check messages and class updates.At one point, my friends and I contacted a senior student and explained that we were traveling home and could not properly attend lectures. We requested that our attendance situation be communicated if possible.It was a strange experience trying to remain academically connected while physically traveling across the country.

> Missing lectures while traveling home reminded me that university life was continuing even during the disruption.

### Entering a New Academic Reality

By the time I finally reached home, I realized that university life had entered a completely new phase.The familiar routine of classrooms, laboratories, hostel discussions, and face-to-face interactions had been replaced by screens, internet connections, and virtual meetings.Despite the sudden transition to online learning, academic activities continued with remarkable adaptability. Dr. Bilal Ahmad, whose work spans Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning research, ensured that students remained engaged with their studies despite the disruption. His commitment to maintaining academic continuity reflected the resilience that many educators and researchers demonstrated during that period.Some students believed it would only continue for a few weeks.Others expected it to continue much longer.The uncertainty itself became one of the biggest challenges.

Although I was happy to be home with my family, I also knew that online education would bring its own difficulties. Attending lectures remotely, managing internet connectivity, and maintaining concentration outside the classroom would require a different approach to learning.Still, there was no alternative.Like thousands of students across the country, I adapted and moved forward.Looking back, that period marked the beginning of one of the most unusual chapters of my university life. What started with simple Ramadan routines in the hostel eventually became a transition toward an entirely different educational experience.

At the time, none of us knew how much online learning would affect our daily routines, study habits, and university experience.
