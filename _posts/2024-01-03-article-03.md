When the admission process ended, I finally received an offer for Computer Engineering at the Faisalabad campus of UET Lahore. Although my original goal had been the Lahore main campus, I decided to accept the opportunity and continue my journey there.

Around that time, severe floods had affected many parts of Pakistan. Because of the situation, universities remained closed for nearly fifteen days. The campus was still not fully active when a few friends and I decided to travel to Faisalabad two days before the official hostel allotment date.

## First impression of the campus

It was my first visit to Faisalabad. Even the rickshaw driver did not know where the university was, and I had to guide him with Google Maps. That made the place feel even more unfamiliar.

When we arrived, the surroundings looked isolated and almost deserted. The campus seemed cut off from the main city. Because it had been closed for days due to floods and vacation, the roads were messy, grass was overgrown, and the environment felt more like an abandoned area than an active university.

> The overgrown campus and unfamiliar surroundings made me question my decision for a moment.

## Hostel worries and fee surprise

The first visit did not allow document submission, so we returned home and came back again on the official date. This time the campus felt alive. More students were present and offices were working normally.

During hostel allotment, another surprise appeared. We were told every student had to immediately submit a security fee of 12,000 PKR for the hostel mess, and we were told to pay it in cash on the spot. None of us had expected that.

I was with my friends Muhammad Zain and Ali Shirazi, and for a few minutes we thought we might lose our hostel rooms. Fortunately, my elder brother had enough cash with him and helped us complete the payment. Later we discovered the fee could also be paid online, but at that moment we had only been told about cash.

## The first real technical moment

In the first Programming Fundamentals lab, we installed Python and Visual Studio Code. For many students that may have been a small task, but for me it felt like the real beginning of my engineering journey.
