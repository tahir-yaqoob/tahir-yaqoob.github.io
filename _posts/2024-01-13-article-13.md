### A Common Student Problem

One of the most interesting aspects of university life is that learning does not always happen through lectures, textbooks, or laboratory exercises. Sometimes the most valuable lessons emerge from ordinary situations that nobody initially considers educational.

During my second semester at UET Lahore Faisalabad Campus, one such lesson began with a problem that every student in the class could relate to: printing laboratory manuals.

For students, printing academic material is often more expensive than it appears. Beyond tuition fees, hostel expenses, transportation costs, and everyday living expenses, there are numerous hidden educational costs that accumulate throughout a semester. One of these was laboratory manual printing.

Students in previous courses had spent approximately 2500 PKR each on printing manuals and related coursework. While this amount may not seem excessive on its own, it becomes significant when multiplied across multiple subjects and combined with other university expenses.

Apart from the financial burden, there was also inconvenience. Students frequently had to visit printing shops, wait for their documents, ensure correct formatting, and return multiple times whenever new submissions were required. As deadlines approached, printing shops became crowded, making the process even more frustrating.

Because of this, students began discussing possible alternatives. Since most manuals and assignments already existed in digital form, many of us believed that soft-copy submissions would be more practical, cost-effective, and time-saving.

Eventually, the entire class agreed to raise this concern with our instructor, expecting a simple approval or rejection.


### An Unexpected Response

When the issue was presented to Dr. Bilal Ahmad, he listened carefully to the students’ concerns. We expected a straightforward academic response about submission rules and formatting requirements.

Instead, he shifted the entire direction of the discussion.

Rather than focusing only on the problem, he began talking about opportunity. He explained that if hundreds of students require printing services, then there is clearly demand. And where demand exists, there is always potential for innovation and business.

His suggestion surprised the entire class.

He proposed that a group of students could pool their resources, purchase a printer, and offer printing services to other students on campus. Instead of seeing printing as a financial burden, we could see it as a practical business opportunity.

What made this idea even more impactful was not just the business concept, but the mindset behind it. While we were thinking in terms of saving money, he was thinking in terms of creating value.

He also mentioned that if students were serious about pursuing such an idea, he would be willing to support them. This showed that his suggestion was not theoretical but rooted in practical encouragement.

As the discussion continued, he further suggested that technology could enhance the idea. A simple website could be created where students upload files, place printing requests, and collect their printed documents later. This transformed a basic idea into a tech-enabled service model.


### Lessons Beyond Printing

Although the final decision still required printed manuals, a compromise was reached. Students were allowed to submit work in soft form during the semester, while final printed submission would be required only at the end. This reduced repeated printing and made the process more manageable.

Looking back, the most important lesson from this discussion was not about printing at all. It was about mindset.

Most students see problems and try to avoid them. Dr. Bilal Ahmad demonstrated a different approach—he identified opportunity within the same problem. This ability to shift perspective is essential in engineering, entrepreneurship, and innovation.

As Computer Engineering students, we learn technical skills such as programming, databases, and system design. However, technical knowledge becomes far more powerful when combined with creativity and problem-solving thinking.

A printer is just a machine, and a website is just software. But when combined with the right idea, they become a complete solution to a real-world problem.

That realization made this classroom discussion memorable.
