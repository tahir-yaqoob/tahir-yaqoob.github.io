During the semester, another issue arose in our class regarding lab manuals. Many students, including me, were concerned about the cost and difficulty of printing them. Previously, we had spent around 2500 rupees per student printing manuals for another course, and printing was both expensive and inconvenient.

Because of these problems, the whole class decided to speak to our instructor and request permission to submit manuals in soft copy format instead of printed copies.

## A teacher's inspiring suggestion

When we discussed the issue with Dr. Bilal, he gave us a very interesting idea. Instead of only seeing the problem, he encouraged us to view it as a business opportunity. He suggested that four or five students could collectively buy a printer and provide printing services for others in the university.

He even said that if a student wanted to start the idea and had no partners, he would be willing to join and support the initiative. He also suggested making a simple website where students could upload their files and request printing services.

> The suggestion showed how technology and entrepreneurship can solve everyday student problems.

I found that idea very inspiring because it connected education, practical problem-solving, and business thinking in one simple classroom moment.
