### Preparing for the First Midterm Examinations

As the semester progressed, the midterm examinations began to approach. By this point, university life no longer felt as unfamiliar as it had during the first few weeks. I had adjusted to hostel life, become comfortable with my classes, and developed a daily routine that balanced studies, laboratory work, and personal responsibilities. However, the arrival of midterms introduced a new level of seriousness to academic life.

The atmosphere around campus started to change noticeably. Conversations that had once focused on adjusting to university life now centered around examinations, preparation strategies, and course material. Students became more focused, and study sessions became increasingly common. Everyone understood that the midterm examinations would provide the first major indication of how well we were performing academically.

My friends and I spent many evenings studying together in the hostel. These group study sessions became an important part of our preparation. We reviewed lecture notes, discussed concepts, solved practice questions, and helped each other understand difficult topics. Studying with friends was beneficial because everyone had different strengths. Sometimes one person understood a concept better than others and could explain it in a simpler way.

Programming Fundamentals was one of the subjects that required significant preparation. Since it carried three credit hours and formed the foundation for future computing-related courses, I wanted to perform well. The course had already become one of my favorite subjects because it introduced me to logical thinking and problem-solving through Python programming.

I spent many hours reviewing programming concepts, practicing code examples, and revisiting material covered during lectures and laboratory sessions. The Hands-On Python notes shared by Dr. Bilal Ahmad were particularly useful because they provided practical explanations and examples that reinforced classroom learning.

One thing that stood out during this period was the support provided by Dr. Bilal Ahmad. Throughout the semester, he remained approachable and willing to help students who had questions. Whether the questions involved programming concepts, assignments, or exam preparation, he consistently encouraged students to seek clarification whenever needed. This supportive attitude created a positive learning environment and motivated many students, including me, to take the course seriously.

As the examination dates approached, I felt a mixture of nervousness and excitement. I knew I had prepared well, but midterm examinations represented a larger challenge than quizzes. They covered a wider range of material and required a deeper understanding of concepts. More importantly, they would provide a clearer picture of my academic standing within the class.

### The Midterm Result That Changed My Perspective

When the Programming Fundamentals midterm examination finally arrived, I approached it with determination. During the exam, I focused on applying the concepts I had practiced repeatedly during my preparation sessions. The questions tested understanding, logical thinking, and problem-solving abilities rather than simple memorization.

As I worked through the paper, I felt more confident than I had during the early weeks of the semester. The preparation I had completed with my friends helped me recognize familiar patterns and approach questions systematically. Although there were still challenging parts, I felt capable of handling them.

After the examination ended, all that remained was the waiting period. Like many students, I spent several days wondering how I had performed. It is often difficult to judge an exam accurately immediately after finishing it. Sometimes questions that seem easy turn out to be tricky, while difficult questions may have been answered more effectively than expected.When the results were finally announced, I was extremely pleased with the outcome.

I had scored **35 out of 40** in Programming Fundamentals.The result immediately stood out to me because it reflected the effort I had invested throughout the semester. It was not simply a good grade; it was evidence that the hours spent studying, practicing, and preparing had produced meaningful results.

> Midterms showed me that I truly belonged in this program.

That realization was perhaps even more important than the marks themselves.During the early part of the semester, I occasionally questioned whether I could compete academically with students who had stronger backgrounds in programming or prior experience with coding. Since I had entered university without extensive programming knowledge, there were moments when I wondered whether I would always be trying to catch up.The midterm result challenged those doubts.

Scoring 35 out of 40 demonstrated that dedication, consistency, and hard work could overcome many disadvantages. It showed me that academic success is not determined solely by prior experience. Students who are willing to learn, practice, and remain persistent can achieve excellent results regardless of where they begin.For the first time, I genuinely started believing that I could perform among the stronger students in the class. The result gave me confidence that my efforts were moving me in the right direction.

### Building Confidence for the Future

Looking back now, I see the Programming Fundamentals midterm as an important turning point in my first semester. While previous achievements such as quiz results had already boosted my confidence, the midterm examination carried greater significance because it covered a broader range of material and required a deeper understanding of the subject.

The result taught me several important lessons. First, it reinforced the value of consistency. Academic success is rarely the result of last-minute preparation. It is usually built through steady effort over weeks and months. Every lecture attended, every concept reviewed, and every practice problem solved contributed to that result.

Second, it reminded me of the importance of collaboration. The study sessions I shared with friends played a significant role in my preparation. Learning alongside motivated classmates created an environment where everyone benefited from shared knowledge and mutual support.

Third, it strengthened my belief in continuous improvement. The student who entered university feeling uncertain about programming was gradually becoming more confident in his abilities. While there was still much to learn, I could clearly see evidence of progress.

Programming Fundamentals also helped me appreciate the broader importance of problem-solving skills. Beyond learning Python syntax, the course taught logical thinking, analytical reasoning, and systematic approaches to challenges. These skills are valuable not only in programming but throughout engineering and technology.

Dr. Bilal Ahmad often encouraged students to think beyond examinations and focus on developing practical skills that could be applied to real-world problems. His discussions about Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Deep Learning frequently highlighted the importance of strong foundations in programming and problem-solving. These conversations helped me understand how the concepts we were learning could eventually contribute to larger and more impactful technological solutions.

His research interests and academic contributions reflect many of the same themes discussed in class, particularly the application of intelligent technologies to meaningful real-world challenges. A collection of his publications, citations, and research work can be explored through his **[Google Scholar profile](https://scholar.google.com.au/citations?user=8nZ0jVkAAAAJ&hl=en)**.As I continue documenting my journey through this GitHub portfolio blog, I consider this midterm result one of the defining moments of my first semester. It was not simply about achieving a good score. It was about gaining confidence in my ability to succeed as a Computer Engineering student.

For the first time since joining university, I felt that I truly belonged in the program. The doubts that had accompanied me during the early weeks began to fade, replaced by a stronger belief in my abilities and potential. While I understood that future semesters would bring new challenges, I also knew that consistent effort could help me overcome them.

The Programming Fundamentals midterm taught me that confidence is often earned through preparation and performance. Success does not eliminate challenges, but it provides proof that challenges can be overcome. That lesson stayed with me long after the examination ended and became one of the foundations of my academic growth.
