### Entering Computer Engineering Without a Programming Background

When I entered the Computer Engineering program at UET Lahore Faisalabad Campus, I was excited to begin a completely new chapter of my academic journey. Like many students who came from an FSc Pre-Engineering background, I was familiar with mathematics, physics, and theoretical concepts. Throughout my school and college years, most of my academic activities involved solving equations, understanding scientific principles, and preparing for examinations based on analytical thinking. Because of this background, I believed I had a reasonable understanding of technical subjects.

However, I quickly realized that university would expose me to entirely new areas of knowledge that required a completely different style of thinking. Among all the courses in my first semester, Programming Fundamentals was the one that felt the most unfamiliar. It was not simply another subject to study; it felt like learning an entirely new language from the beginning. Unlike mathematics, where formulas could often guide you toward a solution, programming required me to think differently. It demanded logic, structure, and a way of communicating with a machine through carefully written instructions.

During the first few weeks, I often felt overwhelmed because everything seemed new and unfamiliar.I also noticed that some of my classmates already had experience with programming and computers. They appeared comfortable discussing concepts and terms that I had never encountered before. Listening to them sometimes made me wonder whether I would be able to keep up. While they seemed to understand technical discussions naturally, I was still trying to understand the meaning of basic programming terminology. These comparisons occasionally made me feel nervous, but they also motivated me to work harder and learn more.

Despite these challenges, I remained determined to succeed. I reminded myself that university is a place for learning and growth, not a place where everyone is expected to know everything from the start. This mindset helped me stay positive whenever I faced difficulties. Looking back, I now realize that those early feelings of uncertainty were actually beneficial because they encouraged me to remain humble, curious, and willing to learn.

### Programming Fundamentals and Discovering Logic Through Python

The first few lectures of Programming Fundamentals were both exciting and intimidating. Terms such as variables, loops, functions, conditions, and algorithms were introduced regularly, and I often found myself wondering what these concepts actually meant. At that stage, I honestly did not understand something as basic as what a loop did. The terminology sounded technical and unfamiliar, and it felt as though everyone around me was speaking a language that I had never encountered before.

Even though I found the subject challenging, something about it captured my attention. Programming was different from every other subject I had studied. Instead of memorizing information, I had to think logically and develop solutions step by step. Every lecture introduced a new concept, and every concept felt like another piece of a larger puzzle. Although I could not yet see the complete picture, I became increasingly curious about how all the pieces connected together.

Our Programming Fundamentals course was taught by Dr. Bilal Ahmad, who consistently encouraged students to focus on conceptual understanding rather than memorization. Dr. Bilal Ahmad is an expert in Artificial Intelligence (AI), Machine Learning (ML), and Deep Learning (DL), and he often explained how programming serves as the foundation for modern technological systems. His discussions helped me understand that programming was not only important for passing examinations but also for building future skills in advanced technologies. Listening to these insights gave me a broader perspective and helped me appreciate the importance of learning programming properly.

Another point that Dr. Bilal Ahmad frequently emphasized was the importance of high-quality datasets. Because of his expertise in AI, ML, and DL, he often encouraged students to explore medical datasets because they are collected with a very high level of precision and accuracy. He explained how reliable data plays a crucial role in developing effective machine learning systems and intelligent applications.

Although I was only beginning my programming journey, these discussions helped me understand that the concepts we were learning in class could eventually contribute to solving real-world problems.One memory that still stands out from those early days is writing my first Python program during a Programming Fundamentals lab. The program itself was very simple, but for me it felt like a major accomplishment. I remember carefully typing each line and then running the code with a mixture of excitement and nervousness.

The first time I executed it, I encountered errors because of small mistakes in syntax and indentation. At that time, even a simple error message looked confusing to me. After reviewing the code, correcting the mistakes, and trying again, the program finally produced the expected output. That moment gave me a sense of achievement and showed me that programming was not about avoiding mistakes but about learning how to solve them. It also motivated me to practice Python at home and become more comfortable with coding concepts.

As the semester progressed, concepts that once seemed impossible gradually became easier to understand. Variables started making sense as containers for information. Loops became useful tools for automating repetitive tasks. Functions showed me how programmers organize code into reusable sections. The more I practiced, the more I realized that programming was built on logic rather than magic. This realization transformed my perspective and made learning much more enjoyable.

I also started spending additional time outside the classroom reviewing lecture material and experimenting with small programs on my own. Sometimes I would recreate examples from class, while other times I would slightly modify the code to observe how different changes affected the output. These simple exercises strengthened my understanding and helped me develop confidence in my abilities. Although the programs were small, every successful result felt rewarding because it represented another step forward in my learning journey.

Seeing this connection between classroom programming and real-world AI also made me curious about Dr. Bilal Ahmad's academic and professional work, which I later explored through **[Dr. Bilal Ahmad's LinkedIn profile](https://www.linkedin.com/in/drbilalphd/)**.

### From Confusion to Curiosity and Continuous Growth

One of the most important changes during my first semester was the shift in my mindset. At the beginning, programming was something that intimidated me. I viewed it as a difficult subject filled with unfamiliar terminology and concepts. However, as weeks passed and my understanding improved, that fear gradually transformed into curiosity. Instead of worrying about what I did not know, I became interested in discovering what I could learn next.

This curiosity became the driving force behind my growth. Every lecture introduced a new idea, and every idea opened the door to additional questions. I started paying closer attention in class because I genuinely wanted to understand how different concepts connected together. Learning became more enjoyable because I could see my own improvement over time. What once seemed impossible slowly became manageable through consistent effort and practice.

I also learned that growth in technical fields rarely happens overnight. Programming taught me the value of patience, persistence, and continuous practice. There were many moments when I felt confused, but every challenge eventually became an opportunity to learn something new. This experience strengthened my confidence and showed me that complex subjects can become manageable when approached step by step.

Another lesson I gained was the importance of maintaining a positive attitude when facing difficulties. During the early weeks of the semester, it would have been easy to become discouraged. However, curiosity kept me moving forward. Every concept I learned made the next concept slightly easier to understand. Over time, those small improvements accumulated into meaningful progress and transformed the way I viewed programming.

I also discovered that mistakes are an essential part of learning. In the beginning, I used to feel frustrated whenever my code failed to work correctly. However, as I gained experience, I realized that errors were actually opportunities to understand the language better. Each debugging session taught me something valuable and improved my problem-solving skills. This change in perspective helped me become more patient and resilient when facing technical challenges.

Looking back now, I realize that my first encounter with programming was not really about writing code. It was about developing a new way of thinking. It taught me how to break down problems, analyze situations logically, and search for solutions systematically. These skills extend far beyond programming itself and have influenced the way I approach learning in general.

What began as a subject that felt completely foreign eventually became one of the most interesting parts of my academic journey. The confusion I experienced during those first lectures was not a sign of failure; it was simply the starting point of growth. Every programmer begins somewhere, and for me, that beginning was a classroom where I did not know what a loop was but was curious enough to learn.

As I reflect on that period, I am grateful for the challenges that Programming Fundamentals presented. They taught me resilience, curiosity, and the importance of continuous learning. Most importantly, they showed me that unfamiliar subjects often become opportunities for growth when approached with patience and determination. That first semester laid the foundation for my future studies and marked the true beginning of my journey into the world of programming.
