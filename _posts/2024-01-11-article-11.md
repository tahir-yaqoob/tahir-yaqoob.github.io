When the second semester began, I entered the classroom with excitement and curiosity. Several new subjects had been introduced in our timetable, including Digital Logic Design, Calculus II, Database Systems, Object-Oriented Programming, and Communication Skills. Each subject felt interesting and challenging.

However, at the very beginning of the semester, I had to miss my first Database Systems lab because I had to visit the main university campus for an interview related to a scholarship program organized by the Higher Education Commission of Pakistan.

At the same time, the first database lab and two theory lectures were conducted. In total, I missed almost five hours of database classes, which felt like a major loss because early lectures usually build the foundation of the subject.

> It was a difficult choice, but attending the scholarship interview still felt like the right decision.

After returning to the university, I contacted my classmates to ask what had been covered. Fortunately, they told me that the instructor had mainly explained the basic database setup, and no advanced concepts had been discussed yet. That made me feel relieved because I knew I could catch up.
